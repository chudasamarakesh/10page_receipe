information

Navratri celebrates the triumph of good over evil, as it marks the victory of goddess Durga over the demon Mahishasura. It is a time for spiritual reflection, devotion, and renewal of one's faith.

Navratri is celebrated with regional variations across India. For example, in West Bengal, it coincides with Durga Puja and involves elaborate idol processions, while in North India, it is marked by Ramlila performances.

Navratri culminates with Vijayadashami or Dussehra, which is the tenth day. On this day, effigies of the demon Ravana are burned in many parts of India to symbolize the victory of good over evil.
