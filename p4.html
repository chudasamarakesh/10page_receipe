NAVRATRI DAY-3

Navratri is observed during the bright half of the Hindu lunar month of Ashwin, typically falling in September or October. It celebrates the victory of Goddess Durga over the demon Mahishasura, signifying the victory of righteousness over evil forces. The festival also symbolizes the harvest season and the importance of good harvests for sustenance.Devotees prepare offerings of flowers, milk, sweets, and other traditional items to present to Goddess Chandraghanta. These offerings symbolize purity and devotion. Prayers and Mantras: Special prayers and mantras are recited in honor of Chandraghanta, seeking her blessings for strength, courage, and peace.
